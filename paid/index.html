Root Cause Overview

29 Jun 2026
What Happened

June 29 set two records: highest installs (14,360) and lowest FTT rate (0.25%). Only 36 users made a first trade.

Root cause: Facebook Ads now controls 98.5% of paid install volume, up from 60% on June 23. Facebook’s FTT rate is 0.03% — 3 traders per 10,000 installs. Google Ads runs at 2.7–6.2% FTT but accounts for just 1.5% of volume.

This is not a single-day event. Blended FTT has declined every day for six days straight. It has not exceeded 0.9% since Facebook scaled.

So What — DAU Impact

Paid spend is generating installs that do not convert into traders.

June 29: 14,360 installs produced ~5 traders (2 from Facebook, 3 from Google). June 23, before Facebook scaled: 4,282 installs produced 80 traders. The install count tripled. Trader acquisition dropped 94%.

App stability is compounding the damage. Crash-free rates are 83–89% against a 99.5% standard. Roughly 5,700 users crash daily — across all sources, not just paid. High-intent users from Google may be dropping out at signup or real-account creation due to crashes.

Engagement data confirms the shift. Average session time dropped 33% (485s → 326s). Sessions per user halved (1.73 → 0.83). The user base is growing in size and declining in quality simultaneously.

Blindspots — What the Data Cannot Answer Yet

Spend is unknown. Facebook quality is clear — 0.03% FTT. But without campaign-level spend, we cannot calculate cost-per-trader. If Facebook CPI is extremely low, the cost math may differ from the quality math. Required: daily campaign spend from Facebook Ads Manager and Google Ads.

Retention is unmeasured. FTT captures first trade only. It does not tell us which users return. A source with high FTT but zero Day-7 retention contributes nothing to DAU. Required: Day 1/7/30 retention by source from AppsFlyer Cohort Report.

One dataset is compromised. Google’s FTDs campaign reports 363 FTTs from 178 installs — a 204% rate. Attribution scoping is incorrect. Google’s aggregate FTT (18.6%) is unreliable. Individual campaign rates (3–8%) are sound. Required: AppsFlyer attribution window confirmation.

Organic impact is invisible. We cannot determine whether Facebook is adding installs or replacing organic ones. If total Play Store installs held flat while paid surged, paid is cannibalizing free users. Required: Google Play Console daily install data.
